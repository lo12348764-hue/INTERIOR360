## What are references

References are visual examples that help a designer understand your tastes, preferences and expectations for a future interior.

### Why references are needed

- **Time savings** — designer immediately understands your taste
- **Accuracy** — fewer misunderstandings and revisions
- **Inspiration** — helps form a visual direction

### Where to find references

1. **Pinterest** — the most popular tool
2. **Instagram** — interior studio accounts
3. **Magazines** — print and online publications
4. **Studio portfolios** — ready-made projects

### What to select

- **Colors** — which shades you like
- **Materials** — wood, stone, metal, textile
- **Atmosphere** — cozy, minimalist, bright
- **Furniture** — specific models or styles
- **Lighting** — types of fixtures

### How to create a reference board

1. Save all liked images
2. Group by categories (living room, kitchen, bathroom)
3. Don't be afraid of contradictions — designer will find common ground
4. Comment what exactly you liked

### Reference board example

Here's what's important to indicate:
- "I like this sofa — soft, pastel"
- "I want this lighting — warm, diffused"
- "Like this kitchen — bright, with island"
